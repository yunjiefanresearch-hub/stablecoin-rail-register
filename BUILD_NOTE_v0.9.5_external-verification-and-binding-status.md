# Build note ‚Äî v0.9.5: external verification landed + the `binding_status` axis

## What this release is

An **external primary-source verification pass** (web retrieval against the official instruments) was
delivered for the original-seven-jurisdiction cells ‚Äî the standing liability every prior release flagged
as the one thing the offline build environment could not do without fabricating citations. This release
*applies* that pass to the register, with discipline.

The pass's central finding is the important part, and it became a new architectural axis:

> **Citability is capped by the binding status of the cited instrument ‚Äî independent of whether the
> official text was located.** A blanket promotion to `resolution_text` would have been wrong.

Three buckets:

| binding status | meaning | tiering |
|---|---|---|
| `in_force_enacted` | enacted statute, in force now | **resolution_text-eligible** |
| `made_not_commenced` | made/passed, not yet commenced (UK SI 2026/102 ‚Üí 2027-10-25) | held at firm_summary |
| `finalized_policy_pending` | finalized policy, not legislated (SG MAS SCS framework) | held at firm_summary |
| `pending_proposal` | bill / NPRM / consultation (US CLARITY, OCC NPRM, BR BCB) | held at firm_summary |
| `prohibition` / `no_regime` | no positive permitted-activity rule (CN) | stays unset |

## What changed

- **New `binding_status` axis** on every record (152), enforced: `resolution_text` requires
  `in_force_enacted`. This is what keeps the citable subset honest even after the text is found.
- **`analysis/verification_ledger.json`** ‚Äî the audit trail (cell ‚Üí instrument, binding_status, official
  URL, pinpoint, verdict, applied tier), generated by `scripts/apply_verification.py`, cross-checked by
  the build against drift.
- **Citable subset 21 ‚Üí 36.** Promoted to `resolution_text` with official URLs: **EU MiCA** (Art. 50
  yield; Art. 58(3) monetary sovereignty; Titles III/IV authority), **US GENIUS** statutory parts
  (Sec. 4(a)(11) yield; the securities carve-out; the Sec. 4(a)(1) 1:1 reserve; the GENIUS issuer
  pathway), and **HK Cap. 656** (eight statutory cells). Held below `resolution_text`: UK SI 2026/102
  (made-not-commenced), SG MAS SCS-specific cells (finalized policy), US CLARITY/NPRM cells.

## Two material corrections from the pass

1. **UK time engine.** The systemic-regime commencement event was modelled at the year **2027-01-01**;
   the gazetted full-commencement day for SI 2026/102 is **2027-10-25**. Corrected (`precision: day`).
   `US‚ÜíUK` now flips `T ‚Üí I` at 2027-10-25, ~10 months later than before.
2. **EU C7 pinpoint.** The non-EU-currency EMT means-of-exchange cap was labelled bare **Art. 23**; the
   correct pinpoint is **Art. 58(3)** (which applies Art. 23 *mutatis mutandis*). This reconciles the
   cell with the SIGNALS basis and the corridor records, which already cited Art. 58(3).

## Deep-review judgments (where naive application would have introduced errors)

- **SG is not pre-regime.** The verification says "SG SCS cells overstate in_force." But naively
  downgrading *all* SG cells ‚Äî including `issuer_pathway` ‚Äî to `proposed` would flip SG into the
  `pre_regime` set (the substrate's pre_regime cross-check keys on `issuer_pathway.status==proposed`),
  which is wrong: SG has an in-force PS Act DPT regime. Only the **SCS-specific requirement cells** were
  moved to `transitional` + `finalized_policy_pending`; the PS Act baseline stays in force.
- **The US reserve cell was split, not over-promoted.** The cell bundled the enacted statutory 1:1
  (Sec. 4(a)(1)) with the proposed 12 CFR ¬ß15.11(b) permissible-asset list (an OCC NPRM). The citation
  now points only to the in-force statute; the NPRM detail is flagged as a pending overlay (the v0.7.1
  claim-purity discipline applied to a pending rule).
- **Original-language jurisdictions were not over-claimed.** CN/KR/TW/BR were verified at the regime-
  status level only (no official-language line-read), so none were promoted to `resolution_text` beyond
  what the in-force AML/user-protection layer supports. Their `binding_status` is recorded; their tiers
  are not inflated.

## Honest boundary (unchanged in kind)

The **27 still-`unset` `tier1_legal` cells** that remain (down from 57) are the residual backlog ‚Äî
CN prohibition cells (correctly unset), and the cells the pass confirmed only at regime-status level or
left for an official-text line-read (e.g. the EU reserve RTS pinpoint, the SG/HK baseline URLs). The
`compose()` results still rest on the hand-curated signal table; the verification narrowed, but did not
eliminate, that dependency. `binding_status` now makes *why* a cell is or isn't citable explicit and
queryable.

## Verification

Full pipeline green offline (builtin validator). `binding_status` gate and ledger cross-check enforced.
Negative tests: promoting a `pending_proposal` cell to `resolution_text` fails; ledger drift (stripped
URL) fails. Substrate pre_regime cross-check stays `{TW,KR}` (no issuer_pathway set to `proposed`). The
UK timeline flips at 2027-10-25. Offline fresh-extract reproduces all metrics.
